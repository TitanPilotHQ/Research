# Titan Pilot Research Charter

## Mission
Produce reproducible evidence about supervised AI decision systems in financial markets without converting engineering validation into performance marketing.

## Scope
Decision provenance, model-contract reliability, hallucination containment, deterministic scoring, replay, human approval, provider resilience, operating cost, safety, and governance.

## Out of scope
Guaranteed returns, strategy promotion, personalized investment advice, undisclosed customer data, and studies designed primarily to support a predetermined marketing claim.

## Research principles
1. Methods before results.
2. Forward evidence is not backtest evidence.
3. Operational correctness is not alpha.
4. Rejections and negative findings are published.
5. Every number has a denominator and time window.
6. Data and code provenance are documented.
7. Limitations are part of the result.
8. Corrections remain visible.

## Study lifecycle
Question -> protocol -> preregistered hypotheses where appropriate -> data classification -> execution -> independent check -> draft -> claim review -> publication -> correction/replication.

## Roles
Author, data owner, technical reviewer, claims reviewer, and publication owner. One person may hold several roles early, but conflicts are disclosed.

## Data classes
Public; public-sanitized; confidential aggregate; customer confidential; secret/prohibited. Only the first two may enter this public repository.

## Required artifacts
Research question, method, sample, exclusions, code/config versions, data provenance, results, limitations, contrary evidence, reproducibility instructions, claim IDs, and revision history.

## Publication bar
A report must be understandable without private context, distinguish measurement from inference, and permit a technically competent reviewer to reproduce or challenge the conclusion.

## Correction policy
Material errors create a new revision and a visible correction notice. Retractions preserve the original record and explain why it is no longer relied upon.

## Success
Research succeeds when it improves decisions, exposes limitations, or creates reusable evidence—even when it weakens a commercial narrative.
