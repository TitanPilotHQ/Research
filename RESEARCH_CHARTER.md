# Titan Pilot Research Charter

## Purpose

Define what Titan Pilot may investigate, how studies are governed, and what qualifies as credible public evidence.

## Research Objective

Build the most trustworthy public evidence base for supervised AI decision-making in professional trading workflows.

The research program focuses on decision quality, governance, validation, reliability, cost, human oversight, and reproducibility. Profitability research may occur, but it must never override the evidence standard or become marketing disguised as science.

## Core Questions

1. Can an AI trading thesis be reliably tied to the exact evidence it claims?
2. Does adversarial review improve decision discipline?
3. Can deterministic scoring remain stable across model/provider changes?
4. How often does the system correctly choose no action?
5. What operational failures matter most in supervised AI trading?
6. How do human approvals alter system outcomes and risk?
7. Which governance controls reduce uncertainty without destroying workflow usability?
8. What evidence should a risk committee require from an AI-assisted desk?

## Research Principles

### Evidence Before Narrative

The study question and method are written before conclusions. Results that weaken the company thesis are published or retained with the same rigor as supportive results.

### Forward Evidence Over Retrospective Confidence

Backtests are useful for plumbing and hypothesis generation. They are not proof of future edge.

### Decision Process Over Profit Screenshots

Research prioritizes calibration, validation, refusal behavior, provenance, reliability, and risk-adjusted outcomes over isolated winning trades.

### Reproducibility Proportional to Sensitivity

Public fixtures and code are preferred. Where customer or broker data cannot be public, the publication must provide a sanitized method, cryptographic evidence where appropriate, and a clear reproducibility limitation.

### Separation of Roles

- engineering produces system evidence
- research interprets evidence
- commercial teams use only approved public claims
- marketing cannot rewrite research conclusions

## Research Domains

### AI Contract Reliability

- schema compliance
- citation accuracy
- fabricated evidence containment
- malformed-output rates
- model/version drift

### Decision Quality

- score calibration
- directional vs none decisions
- veto value
- skip outcomes
- regime dependence

### Human Oversight

- approval/rejection behavior
- review time
- reason-code consistency
- automation bias
- override outcomes

### Operational Reliability

- provider latency and failure
- fallback correctness
- scheduler coverage
- restart recovery
- replay equality
- budget enforcement

### Governance and Market Research

- AI usage on trading desks
- risk committee expectations
- regulatory and DDQ trends
- competitor evidence capability
- category adoption

## Study Lifecycle

1. Research question
2. Prior-art and source review
3. Hypothesis or exploratory designation
4. Protocol
5. Data and disclosure review
6. Pre-registration where appropriate
7. Execution
8. Analysis
9. Adversarial internal review
10. Reproduction check
11. Publication classification
12. Claim-register update
13. Scheduled re-review

## Required Roles

A study should name:

- owner
- reviewer
- engineering evidence source
- disclosure approver
- publication approver

Until the team expands, one person may hold multiple roles, but conflicts must be stated.

## Stop Conditions

Stop and do not publish when:

- data provenance is uncertain
- sample exclusions are undocumented
- a secret or customer strategy could leak
- a metric cannot be reproduced
- a conclusion materially exceeds the data
- legal/public-disclosure status is unclear
- generated model text is being treated as factual evidence

## Research Ethics

- no customer data without explicit authorization
- no deanonymization
- no undisclosed simulated data
- no selective deletion of adverse events
- no benchmark designed solely to make Titan Pilot win
- no hidden parameter search followed by “out-of-sample” claims

## Success Metrics

The research program succeeds when:

- product claims have inspectable evidence
- negative findings produce product improvements
- external reviewers can reproduce key results
- customer committees use the reports
- competitors must answer the evidence standard
- publications remain credible even to readers who reject the company thesis

## Governance Cadence

Monthly:

- active-study review
- claim expiry review
- negative-results review
- dataset/privacy review

Quarterly:

- research roadmap
- external reviewer feedback
- methodology updates
- publication retractions/corrections if required

## Amendment Rule

Changes to this charter require:

- written rationale
- effective date
- impact on active studies
- version history

Standards must not be weakened retroactively to rescue a preferred conclusion.
