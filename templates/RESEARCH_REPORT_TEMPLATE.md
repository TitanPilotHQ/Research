# TR-XXXX — Study Title

## Document control
Status, authors, reviewers, date, revision, claim IDs, public-safety classification.

## Abstract
Question, method, sample, principal result, and the most important limitation.

## 1. Research question
State one falsifiable or decision-relevant question.

## 2. Motivation
Why the question matters and what decision the answer affects.

## 3. Hypotheses
Primary, secondary, and null/alternative where appropriate. Mark exploratory analysis.

## 4. System and versions
Commit, environment, providers/models, prompts, configs, validators, datasets/fixture hashes.

## 5. Method
Protocol, inclusion/exclusion, repetition, controls, metrics, stop conditions.

## 6. Evidence provenance
Source artifacts, data classification, sanitization, raw-result manifest.

## 7. Results
Counts before percentages, distributions, missing observations, failures, and contrary results.

## 8. Interpretation
What the evidence supports and what it does not support.

## 9. Limitations and threats to validity
Sample, environment, temporal, measurement, model variance, selection bias, conflicts.

## 10. Reproducibility
Exact commands or procedure, expected outputs, dependencies, access constraints.

## 11. Public claims
Table of proposed claims, evidence levels, wording, limitations, review dates.

## 12. Revision history
Preserve corrections and retractions visibly.
